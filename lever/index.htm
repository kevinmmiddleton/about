üöÄ Introduction

Context & mission

Lever was moving upmarket. Enterprise customers expected automatic HRIS integration, self-serve data migration, and workflows that didn't break when managers were unavailable. But Lever required manual provisioning, Support-dependent candidate uploads that took days, and workarounds for basic approval flows. This friction blocked deals, frustrated IT admins, and created operational overhead for recruiting teams and Lever's own implementation staff. If we couldn't prove Lever could operate at enterprise scale, both in product capabilities and operational efficiency, the upmarket expansion would stall.

The Operational Gap

Enterprise implementations were bottlenecked by manual data migration. Customers emailed bulk candidate files to Support, who queued uploads in Amazon S3. Turnaround time: 3-4 business days. Errors in files meant additional back-and-forth, stretching timelines to weeks. This created massive overhead for Lever's implementation team and delayed time-to-value for customers.

The Product Gap

Enterprise customers needed Lever to work like the rest of their IT stack: automatic provisioning via SCIM, real-time sync with HRIS, and org-aware workflows. Instead, they faced manual user creation, stale data, and broken approval chains. IT admins provisioned users one by one. Recruiters reassigned approvals manually when managers were OOO. The gap was costing us deals.

Key Problems to Solve

Support-Dependent Data Migration

Customers emailed bulk files to Support, who used backend processes to upload via Amazon S3. Turnaround: 3-4 days. Errors extended timelines to weeks. Large migrations created implementation bottlenecks.

Manual Provisioning at Scale

IT admins manually created and updated 50+ users per month. User data went stale between HRIS and Lever. No SCIM support meant Lever didn't meet enterprise security standards.

Broken Approval Workflows

When managers were OOO, approvals stalled or required manual reassignment. Teams worked around Lever by routing approvals outside the system. Time to Hire and Time to Fill suffered.

üß† Strategy & Solution

Building for enterprise scale, not one-offs

We approached Lever's upmarket challenge in two phases: first, prove we could operate efficiently at scale by eliminating implementation friction. Then, prove the product itself could integrate like enterprise infrastructure. This sequencing let us deliver value quickly while building toward long-term platform capabilities.

Phase 1: Operational Efficiency

We started by eliminating the biggest implementation bottleneck: manual data migration. I partnered with the Top of Funnel team to build a self-serve bulk importer that let customers upload candidate data directly into Lever. Drag-and-drop CSV upload, real-time validation, specific error messages for failed rows. This cut migration time from days to minutes and freed implementation teams to focus on strategic onboarding.

Phase 2: Platform Capabilities

With implementation efficiency proven, we built HRIS Sync to solve the product integration gap. I built the Orion platform team from scratch (0 ‚Üí 7 people) and led deep discovery with 12+ CAB customers. We anchored on SCIM as the industry standard and launched with Proxy Approval‚Äîthe feature that directly improved recruiting metrics. This proved Lever could integrate like the rest of the enterprise IT stack.

Key Decisions

Why Self-Serve First?

Implementation bottlenecks were blocking enterprise deals immediately. Building the bulk importer delivered fast value, proved we could execute, and bought time to build the deeper HRIS Sync platform work.

Why SCIM?

We evaluated Merge.dev (faster, multi-HRIS) but chose SCIM for long-term flexibility. Every enterprise IT stack supported it. Customers could self-implement. We retained full control. Used open-source Scimmy.js to keep costs predictable.

Why Proxy Approval First?

Proxy Approval had the clearest path to measurable impact. Recruiting teams cared about Time to Hire and Time to Fill. Approval bottlenecks hurt both. Launching with manager approval proved HRIS Sync solved real workflow problems, not just compliance.

What we actually shipped

These became the reusable building blocks for Lever's enterprise readiness‚Äîproving we could operate efficiently and integrate seamlessly.

Self-Serve Bulk Importer

Replaced the email-to-Support workflow with a drag-and-drop candidate importer inside Lever. Customers uploaded CSV files directly, saw real-time validation, and received detailed error reports for failed rows. Successful uploads completed in minutes instead of days. Customers retained full control over data migration, and implementation teams were freed to focus on strategic onboarding.

User Profiles with HRIS Data

Enriched Lever user profiles with title, department, team, manager, and location synced from HRIS. This gave recruiting teams better context for collaboration, improved reporting accuracy, and enabled org-aware automations across Lever.

SCIM-Based User Sync

Enabled automatic provisioning and real-time sync between HRIS and Lever. IT admins configured Lever once and trusted that user data‚Äînames, managers, departments, locations‚Äîstayed current without manual intervention. Built on SCIM 2.0 standard for predictable integration with enterprise identity providers like Okta and Azure.

Proxy Approval (Manager Approval)

Let managers approve requisitions, offers, and postings on behalf of their direct reports when they were unavailable. If the manager field synced from HRIS, proxy approval worked automatically‚Äîno manual reassignment, no workflow disruption. This directly improved Time to Hire and Time to Fill by eliminating approval bottlenecks.

Approval Request Review Page

Created a centralized dashboard for tracking approval requests across the organization. Managers and admins could see pending approvals, completion rates, and approval history in one place‚Äîimproving visibility and reducing approval latency.

SCIM was table stakes for enterprise IT, but Proxy Approval was what recruiting teams cared about. Starting with manager approval proved HRIS Sync solved real workflow problems, not just checked a compliance box. The Bulk Importer freed Support and Implementation teams to focus on strategic work instead of manual data uploads. Self-service doesn't just improve customer experience‚Äîit multiplies internal team capacity. Every workflow we could shift to self-service paid compound dividends.

The Bulk Importer wasn't my team, but it was critical to the upmarket strategy. Partnering across teams required building trust, shaping requirements without dictating, and coordinating without slowing execution. Enterprise products require this kind of cross-functional orchestration.
