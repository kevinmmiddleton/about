üöÄ Introduction

Context & mission

Rocket Lawyer's Legal Documents product line was the company's core revenue engine, contributing 90% of U.S. revenue. Even small improvements to conversion rates here had outsized business impact. My mission was to find and unlock those gains through systematic A/B testing and data-driven optimization.

Overview

The document creation flow (where users answer questions to generate legal documents like NDAs, leases, and wills) was the primary conversion funnel. Improving gross conversion rates (GCR) here meant more users completing documents and entering the checkout flow, directly driving revenue growth.

Mission Alignment

This work advanced Rocket Lawyer's mission to make legal services affordable and accessible. By reducing friction in the document creation experience, we helped more people successfully complete the legal tasks they came to accomplish.

üéØ The Problem

Where to focus for maximum impact

Conversion rates had room to grow, but the question was where to focus. Desktop vs. mobile? UX changes vs. performance improvements? We needed a systematic approach to identify the highest-leverage opportunities.

Users & Stakeholders

  • Consumers creating legal documents (NDAs, leases, wills, etc.)
  • Mobile users with different needs than desktop
  • Design, engineering, marketing, and leadership teams
  • Enterprise partners relying on the document platform

Key Problems to Solve

  • Identify which changes would actually move conversion rates
  • Understand differences between desktop and mobile behavior
  • Quantify the impact of site performance on conversions
  • Build a repeatable testing methodology for continuous improvement

üß† Strategy & Solution

Test, learn, pivot, win

We developed a structured A/B testing methodology and ran experiments across the document creation funnel. When our initial desktop hypothesis failed, we pivoted to mobile and found the win we were looking for.

A/B Testing Methodology

Established a repeatable process: ideation ‚Üí pitch ‚Üí brief ‚Üí experiment ‚Üí launch. Great ideas could come from anyone: customers, stakeholders, teams. Briefs captured impact, theme alignment, user journeys, and designs before experiments ran.

Desktop Experiment ‚Üí Learning

We hypothesized that hiding the document preview on shorter documents would increase conversions (suspecting users were copying content). The control won by 10%, teaching us that the preview builds trust, shows real-time updates, and demonstrates value before checkout.

"Frictionless" Mobile Experience

The site was responsive but not optimized for mobile. We designed a "frictionless" mobile interview, removing distractions and streamlining the experience. This variant outperformed control by 5% and was productionized.

Performance as a Lever

Built custom analytics tools to link site performance with conversion performance. This revealed that speed improvements in the document funnel directly impacted checkout rates, leading to our highest recorded conversion rate of 2.34%.
